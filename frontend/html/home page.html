About Registrar


The Addis Ababa Science and Technology University office of Registrar and Alumni, comprising of six units is primarily focused on serving faculty and students by offering scheduling, registration, record keeping, and reporting services. The office manages student registration, keeps track of grades and academic records, issues diplomas and transcripts, verifies enrollment and graduation, assigns classrooms, and handles the issuance, renewal, and replacement of student identification cards.

Registration


The registration process at Addis Ababa Science and Technology University (AASTU) is designed to facilitate a smooth transition for prospective students. It begins with the completion of an online application form, where candidates provide personal information, academic history, and their chosen program of study. Applicants must submit essential documents, including academic transcripts and identification, and may be required to take entrance examinations or participate in interviews, depending on the program. Once accepted, students will receive notification of their admission status and must complete the registration process by paying tuition fees, selecting courses, and attending orientation sessions. Upon successful registration, students are issued a student ID, granting them access to university resources and facilities. Key Feature About our University


The Addis Ababa Science and Technology University offers a diverse range of academic programs, including undergraduate, master's, and PhD degrees. These programs span various disciplines, with a strong emphasis on Applied Sciences, Engineering, and Technology. The university is committed to providing high-quality education and research opportunities, equipping students with the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in their respective fields. Students can choose from a variety of specialized tracks, enabling them to tailor their education to meet their career aspirations. The university's curriculum is designed to foster innovation and critical thinking, preparing graduates to contribute effectively to the scientific and technological advancements in society.
